New ways to commit crimes

Some of the new methods used for committing computer crime are as follows:

Attacks on information may be caused by different categories of people with different objectives. The safety of information is endangered by people for pecuniary benefits, revenge or attempt to do so for the heck of it..


False data entry:

False data entry is one of most simple, safe and thus common types of computer abuses. It involves changing data before or during input. This method, also termed as data diddling, is often adopted by persons who are authorised to handle data for a specific purpose, like entry, examination, encoding or trans­mitting data. The false data is entered normally by

  • Forging or altering the input documents
  • Exchanging storage media
  • Avoiding data validation controls
  • Failing to enter data

Impersonation:

Impersonation is a method of computer abuse wherein a person is able to have access to the password or other identification characteristics of an authorised user, which gives him access to the information system. Such an access to the system is misused by the impersonator for damaging the security of the in­formation.

Trojan horses:

The Trojan horse is a program that inserts instructions in the software of the information system to perform unauthorised acts or functions. This type of abuse is very difficult to identify and much more difficult to fix responsibility.

Salami techniques::

The salami technique of computer abuse is based on the premise that the abuse is generally noticed only when the value per abuse is high. In this technique, round-down abuses are more frequent in cases where rounding off of values takes place while dividing two values.
